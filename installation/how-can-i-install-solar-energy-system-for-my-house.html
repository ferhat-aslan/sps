How to mount your solar panel?



Solar panels should face south

Finding Best Location

The most recommendation is to position solar panels facing south. As we are located in the northern hemisphere, the sun is always along the southern part of the sky as it completes its annual orbit.  



Apart from the direction of the panels, the angle of placement is also important. This angle depends on the position of your roof and is almost equal to the latitude of your house. This angle allows sunlight to hit the panels at right angles and generates a high amount of energy. It also makes it easier for snow to slide off the panels in winter.



The roof can also be a self-sloping roof, there are different mounting methods to optimally place the solar panels.



For the majority of property owners in the US, the ideal angle for a solar panel installation is between 30 degrees and 45 degrees, close to or equal to the latitude of your home (on a south-facing roof).


Mounting Panel On Roof


"Many solar panels, come with mounting brackets that you can simply attach to your platform. For steel platforms or roofs, you will need a good drill. If your panels didn't come with brackets, ask the manufacturers if they can be provided or where the best place to get them is. Also, ask how to attach the brackets to your solar panels. When you are ready to install, first secure the front legs of the brackets. Make sure they are perfectly square and centered before securing them with screws or other mounting materials. Once you have meteorological data to determine the Sun's movements in your area, it's time to attach the rear legs. By the way, I realize that the Earth moves, not the Sun, but bear with me for the sake of these instructions."



Wiring


"You will need a junction connector or a fuse combiner box. You will need to connect the solar panels to the conduit. The wires of the panels must be stripped and wired in pairs before being connected to the fuse box mounted on the platform. The stripped wire should be connected to the terminal box. The red wires are connected to the positive terminal and the black wires are connected to the negative terminal. After connection, the wires are fed under the junction box and reconnected to the respective positive and negative terminal blocks."
