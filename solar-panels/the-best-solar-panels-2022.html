The quality of the solar panel is one of the most important parameters for energy production. Even if you use the best inverter and the best battery system and cables, you may be disappointed if your panel does not convert sunlight into electricity in good quantities.

Which criterias should be taken into consideration when choosing a solar panel?


The goodness of a panel refers to its efficiency (i.e. the rate at which it converts sunlight into electricity), output power, resistance to high temperatures, resistance to environmental conditions, material quality, warranty period and lifetime.



How Solar Panels Generate Electricity?


Basically, what happens is that the solar panel system uses photons to separate electrons from atoms. The process of separating electrons from their atoms also creates electricity. Solar panels contain photovoltaic cells made of silicon that convert incoming sunlight into electricity instead of heat. In these solar cells on the panels, direct current begins to form thanks to the sun's rays. Depending on the region or season of use, the amount of energy obtained is measured and connected to homes or workplaces by installing a serial or parallel device.



We can summarize the flow of solar panels for electricity generation as follows;

  • Photovoltaic solar panels absorb sunlight - Each solar panel contains photovoltaic (PV) cells. PV cells receive light (photons) and convert the light into electricity.
  • When sunlight hits the solar panel, the PV cells start working, producing direct current (DC) electricity.
  • DC electricity cannot power itself. It is transmitted to inverters, where other solar equipment is located.
  • When the sun's rays interact with the silicon cell, electrons start to move.
  • The movement of electrons is captured by the nodes and starts the flow of electric current in the cables in the panel.
  • The solar cables transmit direct current (DC) electricity to a solar inverter to be converted into alternating current (AC) electricity.



What are the Types of Solar Panels?


Solar panels are classified according to two different cell types. The first one is polycrystalline solar cell solar panels and the other one is monocrystalline solar cell solar panels. Today, polycrystalline cell solar panels are widely produced.


Polycrystalline Solar Panels


Polycrystalline panels are more cost-effective than monocrystalline solar panels as they do not require a crucible drawing process. In polycrystalline solar panels, silicon is melted in its raw form and formed into square cells. Thus, the production cost of polycrystalline solar cell structure solar panels is more affordable than monocrystalline cell structure. Polycrystalline solar panels have more widespread production due to their low cost. However, the efficiency of polycrystalline solar panels is lower compared to monocrystalline. The efficiency of polycrystalline solar panels is usually 13-16.


Monocrystalline Solar Panels


They are made of high-grade silicon and therefore monocrystalline panels operate with high efficiency. Monocrystalline solar panels have a performance ratio of 15-20. Since monocrystalline solar panels are more efficient, they are often used in space stations, satellites and high-tech products. The biggest disadvantage of monocrystalline solar panels is that they are more expensive.



How to Choose a Solar Panel?


The power of solar panels in watts is the most important feature that affects the price. For example, there is a difference between a 150-watt panel and a 250-watt panel in generating 100 watts of electricity per hour. Therefore, solar panel prices are determined in watts. Since the production costs of large panels such as 250 watts are more economical than panels such as 100 watts, unit costs are cheaper. When choosing a solar panel, it makes more sense to try to buy the largest solar panel module.
